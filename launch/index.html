    Token Launch Guide

    Warning

    Document under construction. Additional resources and diagrams coming soon

    Depending on your project/expectations/goals, items listed may not be applicable. Further, this is not a guaranteed path to launching or running a successful token, and this documentation should never be treated as law. Information below is outlined as a checklist of sorts, actions a builder may want to consider before launching a token.

    Create your concept

    There isn't a right or wrong answer to what projects concept should consist of. Folks are interested in just about anything you can imagine, and there's plenty of room at the table for all of us to eat.

    • Trend tokens -
      • Predict the future - Quantumrun
      • Ride the coattails of success (Mini, Baby, v2, Community, etc.) - CoinMarketCap
      • Create the trend with unique tokenomics or a new meta - Concept Generators
      • Or make a degenerate deep-fried meme
    • Utility tokens -
      • Rewards/reflections, charity, governance, tool usage, play-2-earn

    Create your social profiles

    There should be a gateway (or two) for your community to interact with each other, get in touch with the team, and feel heard as investors. No room for fear, uncertainty, and doubt (FUD) when the team & community members move with confidence, certainty, and conviction (CCC™).

    • E-mail address - Gmail
    • Google Voice phone number (use that ⤴) - Google Voice
    • Telegram account and channel (use that ⤴) - Telegram
      • Start setting up Rose (filters, chat settings) - Rose Guide
      • Find a couple moderators
    • Twitter - Twitter
    • Discord

    Create your website

    There are many factors to consider when building your site; representation, usability, a11y, branding, UI-UX, etc. Take your time. Present the project how you intend for it to be presented.

    • Purchase domain name - Name.com , Google Domains
      • Be sure to protect registrar
      • Optionally get domain/hosting from same provider; many offer WYSIWYG editors
        1. GoDaddy
        2. SquareSpace
        3. Wix
    • Set up hosting (if separate) - AWS
    • Design and Build your website (details, socials, roadmap, tooling, etc.) -
      • SPA Hosting
        1. Github Pages:
        2. Amplify
      • De-Fi Integrations
        1. Web3
        2. WalletConnect

    Create your roadmap

    Set realistic goals for your project and make detailed plans to achieve them.

    • Utilities -
      • Lottery, NFTs
      • Staking, Rewards calculator
      • Telegram/Discord/Twitter bots
      • White Paper (if applicable)
    • Marketing -
      • Only share top-level strategies (don't show all the cards)
      • Traditional cryptosphere advertising (shilling, calls, ads, etc.)
      • Real-world presence (charity, merch, billboards/flyers, etc.)
    • Milestones & Goals (realistic metrics)

    Create your artwork

    One of the most important parts of a successful project is the branding. Advertisements and artwork need to be eye-catching (at least for the type of eyes you're hoping to catch).

    • Palette for marketing materials - Coolors
    • A set of emojis that embody your theme - Picker
    • Logo, banners, message thumbnails - DALL·E 2
    • Sticker pack(s) and memes - Telegram Sticker Bot
    • Fancy motion graphics - Fiverr
    • Art for potential NFTs

    Create your contract(s)

    Attention to detail is critically important during the contract development phase. Ensure your features have been well-tested, and don't become another exploit statistic.

    • Decide on tokenomics and liquidity amount -
      • Unique features tend to do well
      • Long-term, would be wise to hold a small % of supply in deployer wallet
        1. Fund widget/app pools
        2. Giveaways, aidrops, competitions, chat games
    • Decide on anti-abuse measures -
      • Low max hold, even lower max amount transferred per transaction
      • Blacklist txs within X blocks of launch block
      • Let potential searchers surrender their trades
      • Gradually lower sell tax post-launch
    • Include your social links, ASCII masterpieces, and easter eggs in the comments
    • Finish any other utility contracts that need to be prepared at launch
    • Triple-test everything
    • Ensure commonly used tools show no red flags (post-launch) -
      • SafeAnalyzerBot
      • TokenSniffer
      • Moonarch

    Create your marketing material

    Get ready to present your project. Have informative descriptions/details pre-written for quick posts and rapid sharing.

    • Short, medium, and long format copy-texts, variations with and without links -
      • Be brief and genuine, don't oversell yourself or appear desperate for eyes
    • Scheduled tweets/posts for steady engagement
    • Lore/larp -
      • Anonymous team
      • Link some whale wallets
      • "For the culture", "Based", "King", etc. You get the point
    • Script options for potential AMAs

    Create your marketing plan

    Another critically important phase of a healthy project launch is outlining a detailed marketing plan. You're steering the ship; you should know where it's headed... and when.

    • Find a trustworthy group of flooring enthusiasts
    • First pump-to-floor should be from word-of-mouth ONLY
    • Group attitude should highlight the importance of working for your bags, developers are not here to bail you out -
      • Respectful, honest, clean, clear, and concise communication
      • No tolerance for violence, racism, bigotry, or nudity
      • Remember that your users & community are a large part of your marketing presence
    • Various competitions, 0% tax bonanzas, active participant giveaways - Gleam
    • Play chat games with the community. Bots, polls, media and such, all create entertaining content and can support your project concept
    • Host or get involved in AMAs
    • Costly options -
      • Calls should be spaced to bring the right volume, at the right time, for the right price. We'd advise against calling your project on launch day (if they can't hold 24hrs, they won't)
        1. Callers rankings - BSC Grow
      • 'SHILL Team Six' (twitter and telegram sers). There are plenty of services you can purchase for a reasonable price. Take care to make sure you choose correctly
      • Message-board posts: De-Fi Subreddits , 4chan , etc.
      • Relevant Ad Spaces: Cameo , 4chan , Poocoin , Moonarch
      • Farm coin-voting websites
    • Deliver on roadmap items and share news of advances
    • Put everything on the calendar

    Create your launch plan

    Regardless of scope, nobody wants their token chart to be pumped and dumped from the jump. Take your time and pay great attention to detail when constructing your launch formula.

    • Be ready with a handful of releases to space out over launch week -
      • Degenerates need to see new developments every day, or they lose interest/confidence
      • Create mini-marketing plans to share new developments (shill raids, community posts, etc.)
    • Determine whether it will be a public/private presale, or fair launch -
      • Trusted presaling options - Pinksale , Gempad
    • Do a dry-run on the test network, ensure that your contracts are entirely functional
    • Pick a time and date, start the clock - Embeddable Countdown
    • Make sure your flooring enthusiasts are ready

    Create your token

    Double check everything you've done up to this point, and everything you plan to do once trading is live. There's no going back (unless you launch a v2 LOL)

    • Fund a fresh wallet address with liquidity and deployment funds from a CEX
    • Deploy contract early and unannounced to deter fakes -
      • Verify contract bytecode at the last minute to deter snipers
      • Perform anti-abuse actions
    • Lock liquidity pool tokens immediately after adding liquidity - Hibiki Locker
    • Configure telegram bots -
      • Update Rose's settings for chat, filters for contract, liquidity, tokenomics, swap, etc.
      • Add and configure buy alert & pricing bots
        1. Bog Bot
    • Try to be present in your groups chat while people are excited -
      • Poll to see what your community wants
      • Share previews of upcoming releases
      • Host events (AMAs, giveaways, games, etc.)
    • Continue with your marketing plan
    • Work on your roadmap items

    Success

    Cheers, to building in the bear market!
